The Lion and the Mouse

One bright morning, deep inside a jungle, a mighty lion was napping under a shady tree. As he slept, a little mouse scurried around, playing and chasing leaves blown by the wind.

Suddenly, the playful mouse ran over the lion's paw. The lion woke up with a loud roar and trapped the tiny creature beneath his huge paw.

"How dare you disturb my sleep, little one?" growled the lion, ready to eat the frightened mouse.

Terrified, the mouse pleaded, "Oh mighty lion, please forgive me! If you let me go, I might be able to help you one day."

The lion laughed heartily. "How could such a tiny creature ever help me, the king of the jungle?"

But feeling generous, the lion lifted his paw and let the mouse scurry away.

Days passed, and the lion forgot about the encounter. But one afternoon, while wandering through the forest, he fell into a hunter's net.

The lion struggled and roared loudly, but the more he tried, the tighter the net became around him.

Hearing the lion's desperate roars, the little mouse rushed to the scene.

Without wasting a moment, the mouse began nibbling at the ropes with his sharp teeth.

Bit by bit, the ropes began to snap, and soon, the lion was free!

Overwhelmed with gratitude, the lion said, "I was wrong, little mouse. You saved my life, and I will always be thankful."

The mouse smiled and replied, "Even the smallest creature can help a great king."

From that day, the lion and the mouse became the best of friends and looked after each other.

They taught everyone in the jungle that kindness should never be measured by size or strength.

Whenever animals in the forest saw them together, they were reminded of how friendship can grow from unexpected places.

The lion became humbler and often shared this story with others who thought they were too important to be kind.

The mouse too told his fellow mice to never be afraid to help someone bigger than themselves.

And so, the lion and the mouse lived happily, protecting the jungle as a team.

Everyone admired their bond and learned a valuable lesson about courage, kindness, and gratitude.

Moral: Kindness is never wasted.
