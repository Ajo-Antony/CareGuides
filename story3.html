The Boy Who Cried Wolf

In a small village, there was a shepherd boy who watched a flock of sheep near the hills. He found his job boring and thought of ways to amuse himself.

One day, he shouted, “Wolf! Wolf! A wolf is chasing the sheep!” Hearing his cries, the villagers came running with sticks to drive away the wolf.

But when they arrived, they found no wolf, just the boy laughing at them. The villagers were upset but returned to their work.

The next day, the boy played the same trick. Again, he cried, “Wolf! Wolf!” and once more the villagers rushed to his aid.

Yet again, there was no wolf, and the boy chuckled as the villagers grumbled and left.

However, on the third day, a real wolf appeared from the forest and started attacking the sheep!

The boy shouted loudly, “Wolf! Wolf! Please help me!” But this time, no one came.

The villagers thought the boy was playing tricks again and ignored his cries.

The wolf scattered the flock, and many sheep were lost. The boy was heartbroken and regretted deceiving the villagers.

When he returned to the village that evening, he confessed everything to the villagers and apologized.

The villagers forgave him but reminded him that lying breaks trust and has consequences.

From that day on, the boy never lied again and became known for his honesty.

He learned that trust is hard to rebuild once it’s broken.

Even when years passed, the boy often told young children the lesson he had learned the hard way.

The villagers also shared the story as a warning about the dangers of lying.

The boy grew up to be a wise and responsible man, always remembered for the lesson he taught the village.

And so, the village remained a place where honesty was valued above all.

Moral: Liars are not believed, even when they speak the truth.
