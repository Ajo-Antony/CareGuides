The Ant and the Grasshopper

During a warm summer, a grasshopper spent his days singing and dancing in the meadow.

Nearby, a group of ants worked tirelessly, storing food for the cold winter ahead.

The grasshopper mocked the ants, saying, “Why do you waste the summer working so hard? Come dance with me!”

The ants replied, “We are preparing for winter. When the cold comes, you’ll regret not preparing.”

The grasshopper ignored the ants’ warning and continued to enjoy the sunny days.

Days turned into weeks, and soon autumn arrived. The grasshopper still didn’t gather food.

Then winter came. The meadows turned white, and food became scarce.

The grasshopper shivered in the cold and searched for food but found none.

Hungry and cold, the grasshopper remembered the hardworking ants.

He went to the ants’ home and pleaded, “Dear ants, please share some of your food with me. I am starving.”

The ants said, “All summer, we warned you, but you chose to play instead of work.”

Feeling pity, the ants still shared some food with the grasshopper.

The grasshopper thanked them and realized the importance of hard work and planning.

When spring returned, the grasshopper promised himself to work hard like the ants.

He began helping the ants collect food and prepare for the next winter.

As time passed, the grasshopper became a responsible member of the meadow community.

He never forgot the lesson learned from the kind ants.

From that year onward, the grasshopper balanced work with play and was prepared for every season.

Moral: Always be prepared; work hard today for a better tomorrow.
