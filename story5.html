The Fox and the Grapes

One sunny afternoon, a hungry fox was wandering through the woods.

He came across a vineyard with luscious, ripe grapes hanging high from a trellis.

The sight of the juicy grapes made his mouth water instantly.

The fox jumped and jumped but the grapes were too high to reach.

He tried leaping again, this time with more force, but still failed.

Feeling determined, the fox tried to climb the nearby tree, but it was too slippery.

After several attempts, the fox grew tired and frustrated.

He sat down for a moment to catch his breath and thought deeply.

But soon enough, he stood up and decided to try once more.

Despite all his efforts, the grapes remained just out of reach.

Finally, the fox gave up and turned away from the vineyard.

As he walked away, he muttered to himself, "Those grapes are probably sour anyway."

He convinced himself that the grapes weren't worth having after all.

Other animals who heard him chuckled quietly, knowing the real reason.

The fox's pride didn’t allow him to admit that he failed to get the grapes.

He kept walking, pretending he never really wanted them.

The fox’s disappointment was hidden behind a mask of indifference.

In truth, he was deeply annoyed at not being able to reach them.

The story of the fox and the grapes soon spread through the forest.

It became a popular tale about coping with unfulfilled desires.

Moral: It’s easy to despise what you can’t have.
