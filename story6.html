The Golden Touch

Once upon a time, there was a wealthy king named Midas who loved gold above all else.

He spent his days counting his treasures and wishing for more.

One day, a mysterious stranger appeared and granted Midas a wish.

Without hesitation, Midas wished that everything he touched would turn to gold.

The stranger warned him to be careful, but Midas was too excited to listen.

The next morning, Midas woke up and touched his bed—it instantly turned to solid gold.

Overjoyed, he ran around the palace turning chairs, tables, and even flowers into gold.

However, when he tried to eat breakfast, the food turned to gold in his hands.

He grew hungry and thirsty but couldn’t consume anything.

Suddenly, his daughter came running to hug him, and to his horror, she too turned into a golden statue.

Midas was heartbroken and realized the terrible mistake he had made.

He cried out to the stranger, begging for the golden touch to be taken away.

The stranger appeared once more and told Midas to wash in the river to lift the curse.

Midas hurried to the river and washed his hands, watching as the magic faded away.

He rushed back to the palace and hugged his daughter, who returned to life.

From that day on, Midas valued love and kindness more than wealth.

He shared his riches and treated his people with generosity.

His kingdom flourished, not because of gold, but because of the kindness in his heart.

Midas never wished for gold again, for he had learned what truly mattered.

His story became a lesson to all who valued greed over love.

Moral: Be careful what you wish for.
