The Honest Woodcutter

Once upon a time, there was a poor but honest woodcutter who lived near a forest.

Each day, he would go to the woods, chop wood, and sell it to earn a living.

One day, while cutting a tree by the riverside, his axe slipped from his hand and fell into the deep river.

The poor woodcutter sat by the riverbank and wept bitterly, knowing he could not afford a new axe.

Suddenly, a kind water spirit appeared and asked, "Why are you crying, woodcutter?"

The woodcutter explained his situation truthfully.

Moved by his honesty, the spirit dived into the water and brought out a golden axe.

“Is this your axe?” asked the spirit.

The woodcutter shook his head and replied, “No, that is not mine.”

The spirit then brought out a silver axe and asked the same question.

Again, the woodcutter replied honestly, “No, that’s not mine either.”

Finally, the spirit brought out his old, iron axe.

The woodcutter smiled and said, “Yes! That is my axe.”

Impressed by the woodcutter’s honesty, the spirit gave him all three axes as a reward.

Overjoyed, the woodcutter thanked the spirit and returned home.

He shared his fortune with his neighbors and inspired others to be honest too.

News of his honesty spread throughout the village and beyond.

The woodcutter lived happily and continued to be an example of integrity and truthfulness.

He never boasted about his wealth but always credited his honesty for his blessings.

From then on, he taught his children the value of being truthful in all situations.

Moral: Honesty is the best policy.
