The Crow and the Pitcher

During a hot summer day, a thirsty crow searched for water everywhere.

After flying a long distance, the crow finally found a pitcher with some water at the bottom.

However, the water level was too low, and the crow couldn’t reach it with its beak.

The crow tried to push the pitcher but it was too heavy to tip over.

It then tried to dip its wing into the pitcher but failed.

Feeling determined, the crow noticed pebbles scattered on the ground nearby.

Using its beak, the crow started dropping the pebbles into the pitcher one by one.

With each pebble, the water level slowly began to rise.

The crow continued patiently, adding more pebbles until the water reached the rim.

Finally, the crow was able to quench its thirst and felt refreshed.

Other animals who watched were amazed at the crow’s cleverness.

The crow smiled and flew away, leaving behind a valuable lesson.

Later, it shared the story with its fellow crows so they could learn too.

The crow’s clever solution became a popular tale in the bird community.

From then on, other animals tried to solve their own problems with patience and creativity.

The crow’s determination showed that there is always a solution if one tries hard enough.

The forest creatures admired the crow’s intelligence and spread the story further.

Even the young animals in the forest learned this story as part of their teachings.

The crow became known as the wisest bird in the region.

All who heard the story agreed: when facing difficulties, persistence pays off.

Moral: Where there’s a will, there’s a way.
